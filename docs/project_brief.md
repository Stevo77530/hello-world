# Project Brief — Capt. Stevo’s Crewchange

## Premise
Capt. Stevo, a cybernetic tugboat captain (Digime/Cyberbeast identity), is trapped in a hostile anti-home world where the systems meant to rotate crews and send workers home are now weaponized. The player fights through corrupted maritime-industrial spaces to force a denied crew change.

## Tone
- Dark, industrial, claustrophobic
- Nightmare logic built from normal docks, corridors, gates, and vessel routines
- Dry humor in dialogue and system messages
- Horror pressure without collapsing into parody

## Core Themes
- Denied relief
- Denied home
- Corrupted work reality
- Institutional hostility as a literal monster
- Familiar workplace language twisted into threat

## Enemy Philosophy
Enemies are not random demons; they are distorted extensions of labor systems, staffing pressure, security enforcement, and infrastructure failure.

Initial implementation scope:
- **Dock Corrupted** as the primary regular enemy family
- Additional enemy families deferred until Level 1 slice is stable
- Environmental hazards and skittering lurkers are secondary roadmap items

## World Language / Signal Phrases
These phrases should recur in UI, signage, intercom, subtitle stingers, and environmental props:
- NO CREW CHANGE
- CREW CHANGE DENIED
- RELIEF UNAVAILABLE
- CAPTAIN REMAINS ABOARD
- RETURN TO VESSEL
- HOME STATUS: REVOKED
- TRANSIT INTERRUPTED
- ROUTE INVALID

## Player Fantasy
You are not a faceless space marine. You are Capt. Stevo: a cybernetic maritime worker fighting bureaucracy, infrastructure, and nightmare authority to get relief and return home. The fantasy is survival with purpose, not just body-count escalation.

## Why This Is Not a Generic Doom Clone
- Narrative drive is labor-horror and denied relief, not abstract demon invasion.
- Spaces are maritime/industrial and semantically meaningful (cabin, hatch, gangway, dock gate).
- Enemy and boss themes are institutional and occupational.
- Language systems (intercom/UI/signage) reinforce psychological oppression.
- Progression is framed as forced transit through corrupted routine, not keycard-only arena chaining.
